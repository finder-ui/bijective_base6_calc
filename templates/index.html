A Primer on Numerical Systems

Most number systems we use are positional. This means a digit's value depends on its position within a number. The "base" of a system tells us how many unique digits are used. The value of each position is an exponent of the base, starting from 0 on the right.

Understanding the Bijective System

This system is a bijective base-6 numeration. It is positional, but with two key differences: it has no zero, and its digits run from 1 to 6. Because there is no zero to act as a placeholder, the system "rolls over" differently. After 6, the next number is 11.

Practical Applications

Bijective numeration has significant advantages in computer science for data encoding and generating unique, human-readable identifiers.

Advanced Concepts

The properties of bijective numeration extend into more complex areas of computer science and mathematics, such as cryptography and algorithmic enumeration.
